The first thing you notice when you walk into King's Road Brewing on a Thursday night is the sound. Not the music — that comes later. It's the room itself. Glasses clinking, people talking over each other, that low hum of a place that's alive. That's where this started.

Blue Claw Country Nights kicked off at King's Road Brewing in Haddonfield on March 20th. Greg Jones, Gary Kopervas, and I sat down for a Nashville-style writers in the round — three guys, three guitars, and the kind of songs that don't need a stage to land.

> There's something about playing in a room where people are close enough to hear you breathe between lines. That's when the songs get honest.

We opened with "Porch Light" because it felt right — a song about keeping the light on for the people you love, played in a room full of people who showed up. Greg followed with one of his that I'd never heard live before, and it stopped the room cold. Those are the moments you can't manufacture.

Gary closed the first set with a story about writing a song on his kitchen floor at 2am, and you could feel the whole room lean in. That's the thing about writers rounds — the stories between the songs matter as much as the songs themselves.

## What's Next

Blue Claw Country Nights at King's Road is becoming a regular thing. We're building something here — not just a show, but a room where the music and the people deserve each other. Follow along and we'll see you at the next one.
